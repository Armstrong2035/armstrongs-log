## How This Website Works

This website is a digital garden, not a blog.

### What that means:

Ideas grow over time. A "budding" note might become "evergreen" as I refine it. Notes link to each other. Concepts build on concepts. There's no chronological pressure. I publish when ideas are ready, not on a schedule.

### The structure:

Free Notes contain essays, observations, and frameworks organized by theme. Project Logs document things I'm building (coming soon). ### Why I built it this way:

The site is deliberately minimal. Most blogs optimize for recency over relevance, hot takes over developed thought. I wanted a space where ideas could mature publicly, where half-formed thoughts could coexist with refined arguments, and where the structure itself reflected how thinking actually works: networked, iterative, and evolving.

If something here resonates, reach out. If it doesn't, that's fine too. Not everything is for everyone.
