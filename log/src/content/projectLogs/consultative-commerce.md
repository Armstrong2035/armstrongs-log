I am building the operating system for consultative commerce. That means I am building a set of tools that allow e-commerce merchants to sell their products to customers in a consultative manner. Consultative commerce is an ecommerce paradigm built on the idea that customer needs are unique, and static product pages can no longer cut it. 

Imagine a sales person in the mall, selling you hair care products. They ask about your hair history, porosity, curl patterns and so on. They need to know these things, so that they can use their expert knowledge to give you product recommenations. My mission is to bring build infrastructure that makes this possible in ecommerce. My vision is that this infrastructure is so inutitive, and easy to use for merchants, and website visitors, that it becomes the default for all ecommerce merchants. 

Consultative commers looks like a sales person on your website, who can help website visitors make purchasing decisions. This is not just chatbots. This is everything from semantic search, to personal forms / quizzes, to personalized product recommendations, and even road maps for effective use. 

Consultative commerce is the opposite of static e-commerce. 
The objective is to reduce decision fatigue, and increase customer confidence in their purchasing decisions. And for the merchants, this means higher Average Order Value, higher customer retention, and lower bounce rate. 

## The Primitives: 

Every operating system has apps. And every app has a set of primitives. Primitives are emergent 'features' that allow users to build their own experiences. The Graphic User Interface is a primitive. The file system is a primitive, since it allows users to define how they want their files to be. The terminal is a primitive, since it allows users to define how they want their commands to be. In Ecommerce, the paragraph is a primitive, since it allows merchants to define how they want their content to be. The product page is a primitive, since it allows merchants to define how they want their products to be. The checkout process is a primitive, since it allows merchants to define how they want their customers to purchase their products. And these ecommerce primitives are found in webflow, shopify, wordpress, and other drag and drop builders. 

So when I say primitives, I am refering to the basic building blocks that make consultative commerce possible for every merchant on on earth.
